Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes SEMS racking storm-hardened? +

SEMS uses a proprietary closed-form tubular design with sealed bearing systems that resist water ingress and environmental corrosion. Our engineering is validated to withstand 150+ mph winds and extreme weather events that conventional systems cannot handle.

What is a closed-form (tubular) racking system? +

A closed-form system uses tubular (hollow cylindrical) structural members instead of flat bars or angles. This design provides superior strength-to-weight ratios, better wind aerodynamics, and sealed connections that prevent corrosion and water penetration.

Do SEMS products meet NREL standards? +

Yes. All SEMS products are engineered and tested to meet or exceed the rigorous structural requirements set by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory. We provide comprehensive engineering reports and third-party validation.

What markets do you serve? +

SEMS serves utility-scale, commercial, agricultural, and residential markets. Our solutions are deployed across North America and internationally. We specialize in challenging environments where durability and performance are critical.

How does SEMS reduce O&M costs? +

Our sealed bearing systems and corrosion-resistant design eliminate many common maintenance issues. The 50-year design life means fewer replacements, and the pre-kitted delivery reduces installation errors and on-site labor costs.

Where are SEMS products manufactured? +

SEMS products are manufactured in the United States, specifically in our Scranton, Pennsylvania facility. We're proud to support American manufacturing and supply chain reliability.
